## Executive Summary

The site has a decent foundation (JSON-LD is present, OG tags exist, domain is clean), but **three critical bugs are leaking every SEO signal to the wrong place**, and the live copy is the *old* template, not the new brand-voice-correct version already staged in Webflow. For an AI-era creator site, the page is also missing the specific hooks that ChatGPT Search, Perplexity, and Google AI Overviews actually use to decide who you are.

**Top 3 priorities (in order):**
1. **Fix the broken canonical URL + JSON-LD `url` fields** — every indexed page currently tells Google "the real me lives at repsandreels.com" (a domain you don't own). This is a one-line fix that unblocks everything else.
2. **Publish the already-built rebrand** — the locked hero ("Two hobbies nobody asked me to combine"), correct SEO title, and correct meta description are all configured in Webflow but haven't been pushed live. Last publish was 2025-12-09.
3. **Restructure your schema for AI-discovery, not just Google SEO** — add Organization/Person/CreativeWork with explicit `knowsAbout`, `audience`, and `sameAs` fields LLMs index. Add an `llms.txt` and a machine-readable media kit.

**Overall:** Foundation is there, execution has regressed. Nothing is catastrophic — most of this is ~2 hours of backend work for a 3-4x improvement in how cleanly AI tools can describe you to brands.

## 1. Critical technical issues (fix this week)

### 1.1 🚨 Canonical URL points to wrong domain
**Live markup:**
```html
<link href="https://www.repsandreels.com" rel="canonical"/>
```
**Problem:** The domain is `repsreels.com`. `repsandreels.com` is a typo/placeholder. Every page on the live site is telling Google "the authoritative version of this page is at a different domain." This is **the single biggest thing hurting your rankings**.

**Same bug in the JSON-LD:**
```json
"url": "https://www.repsandreels.com"
```
Appears **3 times** in the structured data (Person.url, Person.brand.url, and the implied canonical).

**Fix:** Update Webflow site settings → SEO → canonical. Update all JSON-LD `url` fields to `https://www.repsreels.com`. Republish.

### 1.2 🚨 Sitemap.xml returns 404
```
GET https://www.repsreels.com/sitemap.xml → 404
```
Webflow auto-generates sitemaps, so this should be on. It's either disabled in site settings or blocked. Without it, Google/Bing/LLMs can't discover pages efficiently.

**Fix:** Webflow Dashboard → Site Settings → SEO tab → toggle "Auto-generate sitemap" ON. Republish. Confirm `/sitemap.xml` returns XML.

### 1.3 🚨 Robots.txt is empty
Returns 200 but no body. Should explicitly allow AI crawlers:
```
User-agent: *
Allow: /

User-agent: GPTBot
Allow: /

User-agent: ClaudeBot
Allow: /

User-agent: PerplexityBot
Allow: /

User-agent: Google-Extended
Allow: /

Sitemap: https://www.repsreels.com/sitemap.xml
```
**Why this matters for a creator:** LLMs respect these opt-in signals. If you want to show up when a brand manager asks ChatGPT "who are some hybrid creators in the fitness/bagpipe space?" you need the answer engines to have crawled you recently.

### 1.4 🚨 The rebrand is built but not published
- **Webflow page settings (backend):** Title = "Reps & Reels — A piper and a lifter. One feed." ✅
- **Live site:** Title = "Reps & Reels — Bagpiping, Weight Training & Creator Journey" ❌
- **Last published:** 2025-12-09 (4+ months stale)

The new brand voice work, the locked hero, the new meta — it's all staged. It's just not live. This is likely the single highest-leverage action on the list.

**Fix:** Before publishing, resolve the canonical bug (1.1) so the rebrand doesn't go live with it.

## 3. AI discoverability audit (the part you flagged)

For a creator whose primary distribution is Instagram + TikTok, the website's real job is to be **the canonical machine-readable description of who you are**. When a brand manager asks ChatGPT/Claude/Perplexity "find me a hybrid creator who does both fitness and bagpipes," those tools should surface *you* with an accurate profile. Here's the current state:

### 3.1 What you have ✅
- **JSON-LD with `@graph`** containing Person + Brand + FAQPage. This is already better than 90% of creator sites.
- **FAQPage schema** is well-formed — answers are substantive, which AI answer engines love. These become the "voice" of the site when quoted.
- **sameAs** links to IG and TikTok.
- **Person.jobTitle** is explicit.

### 3.2 What's missing or weak ❌

**a) `sameAs` is thin.** Currently only IG + TikTok. Add any of these you own:
- YouTube channel (if any)
- Spotify/Apple Music artist profile (if any)
- Substack/Newsletter landing page
- Strava (fitness credibility for brand partners)
- X/Twitter
- LinkedIn-for-R&R (if you create one — per brief, keep personal LinkedIn separate)
- Any external interview/podcast feature URLs

The more `sameAs` edges, the more confidently an LLM will say "yes this is one entity."

**b) No `knowsAbout` array.** This is the AI-era equivalent of meta keywords, and LLMs actually use it. Add:
```json
"knowsAbout": [
  "Bagpiping", "Great Highland Bagpipe", "Pipe Band", "Snare Drum",
  "Strength Training", "Powerlifting", "Bodybuilding",
  "Content Creation", "Short-form Video", "CRM Marketing",
  "New England", "Gibson Bagpipes"
]
```
This gives ChatGPT/Perplexity explicit topic anchors when someone searches by skill.

**c) No `audience` or `makesOffer`.** Brand partnerships are your stated goal — make it machine-readable:
```json
"makesOffer": {
  "@type": "Offer",
  "name": "Brand partnerships and creator collaborations",
  "description": "Sponsored content, product features, and creator campaigns in the fitness and bagpiping space.",
  "availability": "https://schema.org/InStock"
}
```

**d) `description` is voice-inconsistent.** Live Person.description uses "creator journey" — banned word per voice guide. Fix to match brand voice: *"Competitive lifter and bagpiper posting the overlap. Instagram and TikTok @repsreels."*

**e) No `llms.txt` file.** This is becoming a de-facto standard (similar to robots.txt but for LLM context). A well-formed `/llms.txt` at the domain root tells AI tools what your site is about in plain language. Recommend creating one (see §6 below).

**f) No Video/CreativeWork schema on the reels grid.** When you wire up the CMS-driven reels in the rebrand, each embedded video should emit a VideoObject schema block so Google Video search, YouTube-of-others, and TikTok-Search-via-Google can index individual posts.

**g) No OG image at root path.** Currently served from CDN — fine, but `og-image.png` at `https://www.repsreels.com/og-image.png` is the convention LLMs check first.

### 3.3 AI-answer-engine readiness score (rough)

| Dimension | Score | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Crawler access | 🟡 3/10 | Empty robots.txt, no sitemap |
| Entity clarity | 🟢 7/10 | Person + Brand graph is solid |

## 5. Keyword + content-gap analysis (creator-context)

Per your SEO package, the strategy is branded-only — don't chase "bagpipes Instagram" or "hybrid athlete" keywords. I agree with that strategy, with one refinement for the AI era:

**Branded + AI-discovery keyword opportunities:**

| Query intent | Example phrase | Volume | Difficulty | How to capture |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Direct branded | "Reps and Reels" / "repsreels" | Low-medium | Easy | Already targeted — canonical fix unblocks |
| Entity disambiguation | "Steve Hebert bagpiper" | Low | Easy | Person schema with full name + location |
| Niche intersection | "bagpiper who lifts weights" / "hybrid creator bagpipes fitness" | Very low but zero competition | Easy | FAQ-style content answering exactly this |
| Brand-partnership intent | "bagpipe brand ambassador" / "fitness creator for brand partnership New England" | Very low | Easy | Explicit `makesOffer` schema + media kit page |
| Gear credibility | "Gibson bagpipes review" | Low | Hard | Quick-win: one gear page mentioning your kit (bagpipes, drums, barbell, shoes) |

**The one piece of content I'd add:** a `/work-with-me` or `/partnerships` page. Currently no page on your site explicitly targets a brand marketer searching for creators. Add one with:
- Audience stats (pulled from IG/TikTok insights, refreshed quarterly)
- Past brand work (if any) — even unpaid features count
- What you'll and won't do (protein powder OK, diet pills no — your call)
- Contact CTA

This is the single page most likely to be cited by an AI tool when a brand asks about you.
